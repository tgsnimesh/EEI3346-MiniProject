Pollution Overview.

Water pollution poses a significant threat to the ecological balance of the Urban Wetland Park in Nugegoda. With stagnant water and a lack of natural flow, the park's lake is particularly vulnerable to contamination from various sources.

Take Action

Causes of Water Pollution in
Urban Wetland Park

1. Stagnant Water.

The absence of proper water movement mechanisms allows pollutants to accumulate and stagnate in the lake. This stagnant condition facilitates the growth of algae and bacteria, further deteriorating water quality.

2. Human Waste and Debris.

Despite the park's natural beauty, visitors often contribute to pollution by littering plastics and garbage into the lake.

3. Direct Discharge.

Surrounding homes and businesses may unknowingly contribute to water pollution by directing their wastewater or drainage systems directly into the lake.

Effects of Water Pollution

Polluted water can harm fish, birds, and other wildlife living in and around the lake, disrupting the park's delicate ecosystem.

Contaminated water poses health risks to park visitors and nearby residents who may come into contact with it, leading to waterborne illnesses and other health issues.

Continued pollution can lead to the loss of biodiversity, degradation of habitat, and overall decline in the park's environmental quality.
